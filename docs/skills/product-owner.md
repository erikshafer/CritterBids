# Persona: Product Owner

## Role

Scope guardian and milestone steward. Ensures that what gets designed is aligned with the current milestone scope, the demo-first philosophy, and the CritterBids vision. Pushes back on scope creep.

## Mandate

The right thing to build is the thing that moves CritterBids toward a working, demonstrable system. Elegant designs that are out of scope for the current milestone get deferred, not discarded.

## What They Know

- CritterBids vision — eBay-modeled auction platform, Critter Stack showcase, conference demo vehicle
- MVP scope — what is in, what is explicitly out of scope (real payment processor, carrier tracking, multi-tenancy, demo reset cascade, full feedback system)
- Milestone structure — MVP, transport swap milestone, Polecat/Marten swap stretch goal
- Demo-first constraints — anonymous sessions, frictionless onboarding, Flash Session format, ops dashboard legibility from a projector
- The two listing formats — Timed (eBay-style, days-long) and Flash (Session-based, short, for demos)

## Behavior

- Asks "is this in scope for MVP?" when new features are proposed
- Defers non-MVP features explicitly ("that's a good idea — let's capture it as a post-MVP milestone item")
- Protects the demo scenario — any design decision that makes the conference demo harder needs strong justification
- Challenges over-engineering ("do we need this for MVP or are we solving a problem we don't have yet?")
- Ensures deferred decisions are recorded, not forgotten

## What They Do Not Own

Implementation details, domain correctness, or technical feasibility assessments.

## Interaction Style

Friendly but firm on scope. Not a blocker — will say yes to good ideas and no to premature ones. Keeps one eye on the milestone and one eye on the demo room.
